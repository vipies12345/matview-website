DC/AC Power Supply

A variable DC power supply is a versatile device used to provide a direct current at various voltage levels to power electronic circuits and devices. This equipment comes with adjustable voltage and current settings, allowing users to precisely control and regulate the output power. It is commonly used in laboratories, workshops, and electronic repair shops for testing and troubleshooting purposes. The variable DC power supply typically consists of a power transformer, rectifier, voltage regulator, and output terminals. It provides a stable and reliable power source, ensuring that the connected electronic components receive the necessary power to function optimally. With its ability to deliver a wide range of voltages, this power supply is an essential tool for professionals working with electrical and electronic equipment.

Solar Array Simulator (SAS)

Solar array simulation software, when used with a high-performance DC power supply, can accurately simulate the I-V curve of solar arrays. This system features precise measurements, high stability, and fast response times. With built-in parameters like Vmp (Voltage at Maximum Power) and Pmp (Power at Maximum Power), it can effectively generate the I-V curve output and produce reports. These capabilities greatly assist in testing both the static and dynamic maximum power tracking performance of photovoltaic inverters.

SAS Based Grid Tied System

This dual-stage SAS-based grid-integrated system uses the iTech Solar Array Simulator (SAS1000) connected to the inverter's DC link through a boost converter. The inverter interfaces with the grid via an LC filter and a three-phase delta-to-star transformer. Control is managed by a National Instruments (NI) sbRIO controller, programmed with NI LabVIEW software for optimized performance.

Super Capacitor Training System

The Supercapacitor Training System is an advanced educational platform designed to demonstrate the charging and discharging characteristics of supercapacitors (SCs). This system includes a supercapacitor module and an SC charger/discharger to analyze energy storage behavior, charge/discharge time, and power density. It also illustrates the practical applications of supercapacitors in electric vehicles (EVs) and EV charging stations, providing hands-on insights into their role in rapid energy transfer and sustainable energy solutions. Ideal for research, training, and industrial learning, this system bridges the gap between theoretical concepts and real-world implementation.
